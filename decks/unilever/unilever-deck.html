03 · A New Era
How we're thinking about sustainability today
Three shifts that change how Unilever delivers on its commitments — more focused, more integrated, more systemic.

We're prioritising sustainability actions where we can have the biggest impact, with 15 stretching near- and medium-term goals across our four priority areas: climate, nature, plastics, and livelihoods.

Our progress depends on wider system changes. We're using our influence and working with key decision-makers across business, government, and society to remove barriers to progress.

"We're hard-wiring sustainability into our strategy, governance, and operations — leaning pragmatically into challenges to make progress, step-by-step."
Unilever Sustainability Approach
04 · The Three Shifts
Three shifts in our approach
More focus. More integrated. More systemic. Same commitments — delivered differently.
What changed
Tighter focus on the impact that matters
  • —Concentrate effort on climate, nature, plastics, and livelihoods — the four areas where we can move the needle.
  • —15 near- and medium-term goals replace longer wish-lists. Each is measurable; each carries an owner.
  • —Hard-wire sustainability into strategy, governance, and operations rather than parallel programmes.
Why now
The next decade is not a continuation of the last
  • —The world must move further, faster to avoid the worst effects of climate change.
  • —Plastics, regenerative agriculture, and human rights now require system-level cooperation — beyond our value chain.
  • —Pragmatic, step-by-step delivery beats ambition that quietly slips behind plan.
05 · Where Progress Depends on System Change
Direct action ends where the value chain begins
The four priority areas, mapped against what we control inside Unilever versus where partners and policy carry the rest of the load.
Priority What we control directly Where progress depends on others Posture
Climate Scope 1+2 emissions across our factories, fleet, and operations. Scope 3 with suppliers and consumer use; trade-association climate positions. — System-dependent
Nature Sourcing standards inside our supply chain; brand-led regenerative pilots. Wider regenerative-agriculture adoption; biodiversity policy at landscape scale. — Joint action
Plastics Packaging design, refill formats, and recyclability of our own portfolio. UN treaty outcomes; municipal recycling infrastructure in our 190 markets. — System-dependent
Livelihoods Living wage for direct workforce and key suppliers; human-rights diligence. Living wage policy across the broader value chain; multilateral labour standards. — Joint action
06 · How the Business Earns · FY 2025
Two structural truths shape every commitment
Growth concentrates in emerging markets; profit concentrates in our Power Brands. Strategy that ignores either one is a strategy in name only.

Power Brands share of turnover

R&D as a strategic line item
€836m

What this means
Sustainability has to compound through emerging markets and Power Brands — or it doesn't compound at all.
Climate, plastics, and livelihood gains land hardest where consumption grows fastest. Concentrating execution on the brands that already carry 78% of turnover is how a 15-goal plan becomes a 15-goal delivery.
