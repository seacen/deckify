03 · WHY THE NEXT FIVE YEARS ARE DIFFERENT
The next five years are not a continuation

The first half of the 2030 plan was, in retrospect, the easy half. Scope 1 and 2 emissions live inside our four walls and our power contracts; we could renegotiate, retrofit, and retire our way to a 74% reduction without changing a single recipe. The remaining 76% of our footprint sits in scope 3 — in the soy, the dairy, the surfactants, the cold chains we don't own, and in the hot showers our consumers will take whether or not we ship a more concentrated formulation. Halving that requires us to redesign products, recontract suppliers, and retrain a billion daily users. It is a fundamentally different operating model than the work of 2020–2025.

"We are no longer reducing our emissions. We are reducing the emissions of the companies and consumers we are connected to."

06 · WHAT REFORMULATION ACTUALLY MEANS
What "reformulated" actually looks like
Packaging cross-section · Dove body wash bottle

↓
2026 target
Mono-material PE bottle + PE label
CSS schematic — illustrative

Each transition looks like a small change on shelf and is, in practice, a different molecular contract with the supply chain.

Recycled-PET requires food-grade reclaimed flake at scale — currently rationed across the FMCG industry. Color drift and odour carry-over force tighter QA gates.

Mono-material PE trades barrier performance for end-of-life recyclability. We absorb the shelf-life cost; the recycler gets a clean stream.

Neither is a marketing change. Both are R&D and procurement programmes that take 18–30 months per SKU.
