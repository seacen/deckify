02 / 09 Our Purpose
Beauty for All

Creating the beauty
that moves the world.

Beauty needs vary infinitely depending on the culture, the gender, and the generation. Our purpose is to make beauty accessible to everyone — providing the right product, in the right place, at the right moment.

Across 37 international brands, four divisions, and 150 countries, our scientists, makeup artists, hair colourists, and product engineers work toward a single ambition: that the beauty experience be authentic, inclusive, sustainable, and grounded in rigorous science.

This is what we mean when we say "Because beauty drives the world." Not as advertising — as a working proposition that shapes how the Groupe invests, hires, formulates, and packages.

"We strive to make beauty accessible to all — and to do it in a way that respects both people and the planet."

— L'Oréal Groupe — Our Purpose
03 / 09 Commitments
L'Oréal for the Future

Two horizons.
One commitment.

For the Planet

Steward the climate transition; safeguard nature; drive circularity.

01
Climate. Reducing absolute Scope 1+2+3 emissions, on a 1.5 °C trajectory, with operations powered by renewable energy at industrial sites.
02
Nature. Sustainable sourcing of biobased ingredients; protecting and restoring ecosystems linked to our value chain.
03
Circularity. Refill, recycled and bio-based plastics; eco-designed packaging across every brand.
For the People

Caring for employees; supporting communities; respecting human rights.

01
Employees. Health and well-being benchmarks across every site; living-wage commitment for every direct employee.
02
Communities. The L'Oréal for Youth programme; partnerships with vulnerable women in 60+ countries.
03
Human rights. Strategic-supplier audits; published policies on diversity, equity and inclusion.

07 / 09 Heritage
The Adventure of Beauty

A century, in milestones.

1909
Société française de teintures inoffensives
Eugène Schueller, a young chemist, files the patent that becomes L'Oréal.
1934
Dop, the first soap-free shampoo
A democratisation product — beauty leaves the salon and enters the home.
1957
François Dalle takes the helm
The expansion era begins. International acquisitions reshape the Groupe.
1973
"Because I'm worth it"
L'Oréal Paris launches the line that becomes the most recognised tagline in beauty.
2013
Sharing Beauty With All
First sustainability programme; baseline year for the L'Oréal for the Future commitments.
2024
L'Oréal for the Future
Climate, nature, circularity and people goals — measured, audited, published.
From a single hair-dye patent in a Paris apartment to a Groupe operating in 150 countries — each milestone is a continuation, not a pivot. The throughline is the brief: safe, accessible, scientifically rigorous beauty.

09 / 09 Looking Forward
Looking Forward

The next chapter is already being written.

From the first soap-free shampoo of 1934 to the eco-designed refill formats of today, every era has answered the same brief in a new vocabulary. The constant is the question — can beauty be both more accessible and more responsible?

The answer, for the next decade, lies in science: green chemistry, dermatological precision, AI-assisted personalisation, and packaging engineered for circularity from the first sketch.
