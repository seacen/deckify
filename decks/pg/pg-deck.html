Who we are
Five facts that frame the company

We are the people behind the brands you trust.

P&G operates as one of the largest consumer-goods companies in the world. Our brands serve people in roughly 180 countries, organised into five operating segments — Beauty, Grooming, Health Care, Fabric & Home Care, and Baby, Feminine & Family Care. The portfolio is built on long-running franchises (Tide, Pampers, Gillette, Crest, Pantene, Olay, Bounty) and disciplined innovation that compounds across decades, not quarters.

Slide 04 / 09 · Approach
How we operate
Two ways of building consumer products

A category-leadership model, not a portfolio of brands.

Conventional CPG company

Many brands, optimised for shelf share.

Compete on price and trade promotion in each category.
Marketing spend allocated quarterly against shifting trends.
R&D distributed across many small initiatives.
Brand equity rebuilt with each generation of consumers.
Innovation cycles measured in product seasons.
The P&G way

Fewer, larger brands earning category leadership.

Win the category through superior performance, not pricing.
Sustained brand investment across multi-decade horizons.
Concentrated R&D on high-impact consumer problems.
Equity compounding generation to generation (Tide since 1946; Pampers since 1961).
Innovation cycles measured in years and decades.
Source: 2025 Annual Report — Strategy Letter

Constraint and response

Two long-running tensions, two long-running responses.

I Constraint

Sustainability is not optional.

Climate, waste, water, and nature are the four boundaries within which every brand must operate.

Response · 2030 horizon

Embed sustainability in how each brand designs, packs, and ships.

From recyclable formats in Fabric Care to refill systems in Beauty, the response sits inside the franchises — not as a side initiative.

Source: us.pg.com / environmental-sustainability

II Constraint

Trust is earned per moment, not per slogan.

Two billion homes a day means two billion opportunities for the product to disappoint a real consumer in a real situation.

Response · 188 years

Make every day more than ordinary — at the product level.

P&G's voice is operational, not aspirational: the response is performance superiority on the actual job (cleaner dishes, drier diapers, fewer cavities) — and the next decade of innovation directed at it.
