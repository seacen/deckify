03 · What changed

What changed in Q2.

Premium consumers maintained spend through the macro slowdown — they did not trade down, they doubled down on routine and ritual. Mass-tier consumers behaved differently. They traded down to local private label faster than any of our Q1 elasticity models predicted, with switching velocity nearly twice the modeled rate in laundry and oral care. The result was a quarter where premium overdelivered, mass underdelivered, and the blended portfolio looked superficially on-track while the underlying mix shifted hard.

We need a mass-tier counter-strategy by Q3 close — pack architecture, price ladder, and channel investment. The window to act before share calcifies is roughly one quarter.

The structural problem

Mass tier represents 64% of Q2 volume but only 41% of margin contribution. The bigger half of the portfolio is the slower-growing, lower-margin half — and it is the half losing share to local private label fastest. Premium acceleration cannot indefinitely subsidise mass erosion at this mix.
