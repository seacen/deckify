03 / Diagnosis The Real Friction

The trust gap is doing more work than the integration gap.

Technical integration to Atlas takes under two hours for a competent founder — the SDK is good, the docs are excellent, and the onboarding flow has been localized into Mandarin since March. The real friction is upstream of all of that: convincing a China-based founder that an offshore Delaware C-corp plus US bank rails is the right architecture for what is, today, a regional business. The integration is a one-afternoon problem. The architectural reframe is a six-month conversation, often with a co-founder, an accountant, and a parent who all need to be persuaded in turn.

Trust takes longer to build than infrastructure.

09 / Pricing Tiers Atlas Plans

Pricing tiers, sized for the Greater China builder.

Solo · single founder
  • Delaware C-corp formation, EIN, US bank account included
  • Multi-currency invoicing across 5 currencies, no monthly fee
  • Mandarin onboarding + Mandarin docs, async support only
Default for first-time founders pre-revenue.
Team · 2–10 builders
  • Everything in Solo, plus shared dashboard and role permissions
  • Atlas Tax templates for distributed US / EU receivables
  • Priority queue support during HK / Beijing business hours
For seed-stage teams shipping cross-border revenue.
Scale · 10–50 builders, $1M+ ARR
  • Dedicated success manager based in Singapore time zone
  • Custom invoicing flows, Connect for marketplace payouts
  • Quarterly tax / structuring review with Atlas counsel
Where most cross-border SaaS exporters land by Series A.
Enterprise · custom
  • Bespoke entity structures, multi-jurisdiction holding companies
  • Direct-to-Stripe-banking line, custom treasury terms
  • Named legal, tax, compliance and engineering contacts
For the handful of Chinese unicorns going fully global.
10 / The Ask Council Decisions Needed

Three things we need from the Council.

01
A green light on on-shore data residency for Atlas China builders.
The KYC pinch will not move without it. Engineering scope: one quarter; legal scope: open question we cannot answer ourselves.
02
Hiring approval for two Mandarin-native account managers in Singapore.
The trust conversation is not an asynchronous one. We need humans on calls in the right time zone and the right language.
03
A clear "no, not now" or "yes, plan it" on Alipay integration.
We do not need a launch — we need a posture. The current ambiguity is costing us roadmap clarity in three other workstreams.
Decision needed before EU launch lock-in, end of FY26 H2. After that, attention shifts and the Atlas China question gets re-opened in FY27 — twelve months we cannot afford.
